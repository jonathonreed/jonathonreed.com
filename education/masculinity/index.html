My Work in Masculinity

I’ve been involved in education and youth mentoring for years. At some point I clued into the fact that the passion and skills that I have for working with boys in particular are not extremely common. A lot of people are really good. Of course. But being versed in theory-based critique of traditional masculinity and having a high-level ability to engage and connect with boys is I guess what I would think of as a bit of a niche.

Feeling like I’m tooting my own horn a little bit here but I don’t want to underemphasize the fact that I’m changing boys’ lives. I’m passionate about this, and I’m good at it too. So this is a ongoing collection of the work that I’ve done. No real reason for it to exist, except I might want to have it someday.

Workshops

These are workshops that I’ve either organized or spoken at. Some of them both.

  • ‘The Mask You Live In’ Evening Activity (Camp Arowhon) — July 2016-August 2016
    Developed and ran four sessions on masculinity and wearing ‘masks’ to hide emotion and vulnerability with collectively around 150 boys aged 10-16. Debriefed with the staff to help them process their own experiences and how they could best help their campers with some of the things that had been revealed.
  • Male Staff Homophobia/Masculinity Training Session (Camp Arowhon) — June 2016
    Led a discussion on what is inside and outside the ‘boy box’ and what kind of culture of masculinity we wanted to create for the boys at our summer camp.
  • Masculinity Inservice (Norval Outdoor School) — March 2016
  • Masculinity and Outdoor Education Seminar (Queen’s Outdoor and Experiential Education Program) — December 2015
  • Maskulinity Conference (Queen’s University) — March 2015
  • ‘The Mask You Live In’ Film Screening and Panel Discussion (Concurrent Education Student Association with Queen’s White Ribbon Campaign) — March 2015
