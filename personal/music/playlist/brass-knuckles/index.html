Brass Knuckles

Garage Rock / 2017

I was standing on the subway, cards twisting through my fingers as we left the station and there he was at the end of the platform. Ripped jeans, scar on his worn face and a glint of metal in his hands before he was gone. I flipped my music to garage rock and leaned against the door.

Some pounding rhythms to cut the edge between whiskey and glass. Every card in the deck gets worn out some time, but not before you’ve made your stack and cut your knuckles.
