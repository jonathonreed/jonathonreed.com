King of All Wild Things

Indie Folk / 2013

Inspired by Maurice Sendak’s story ‘Where the Wild Things Are’ and more immediately by the 2009 Spike Jonze film adaptiation. I remember the book as a wild, playful and irreverent glimpse of childhood emotion, framed with a touch of motherly love. I loved it. The film was a little tougher and had lingering elements of phsychological unease that I wasn’t sure what to make of. Liked it but for different reasons than the book.

So this set of songs is inspired by Max, the story of an uncertain king in a place far from home. It’s meant to feel a bit triumphant and a bit lonely, like a kid who’s gotten his way and is all of a sudden completely unsure that’s what he wanted after all.

One of my early playlists. Part of the BOYS WORTH SINGING FOR collection.
